THE OLD WAY IS BROKEN

Your annual pentest left 95% of the year completely untested.

THEY SLEEP. ATTACKERS DON'T.

Traditional manual red team engagements in India cost between ₹3 lakh and ₹8 lakh and take 3–4 weeks to complete. By the time the report lands on your desk, new vulnerabilities have already been published. A new CVE disclosed on Monday is weaponised by Thursday. Your red team engagement ended last quarter.

AUTOMATED SCANNERS MISS THE REAL THREATS.

In India, the top three initial causes of data breaches were phishing at 18%, third-party vendor and supply chain compromise at 17%, and vulnerability exploitation — none of which automated scanners reliably detect in realistic attack chain context. Scanner tools find known CVEs. They don't find business logic flaws, AD misconfiguration chains, or multi-step lateral movement paths that require reasoning — not just pattern matching.

A SNAPSHOT ISN'T A SHIELD.

Most Indian organisations take 263 days on average to detect and contain a breach — while CERT-In now mandates reporting within 6 hours of detection. That gap between detection reality and regulatory requirement is where liability lives. A pentest done once a year covers approximately 14 days of your 365-day exposure window. The other 351 days, you're flying blind.

Enterprise IT firms charge 3 to 5 times more than boutique firms for the same scope, while freelance pentesters charge 30 to 50 percent less but typically don't provide audit-grade reports.
